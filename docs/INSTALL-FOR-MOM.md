# How to install and use Yoto Maker 🎵

This guide is for **anyone** — you don't need to know anything about computers.
Just follow the steps in order. It takes about 5 minutes to set up, and you only
set up once.

> 💡 If you get stuck on any step, take a screenshot and send it to the person
> who made this for you. There's nothing here you can break.

---

## Part 1 — Install it (one time)

### Step 1: Download the app

1. Go to this page: **https://github.com/mmackelprang/yoto-maker/releases/latest**
2. Under the word **“Assets”**, click the file called **`YotoMaker.exe`** to
   download it.
3. When it finishes downloading, find it (usually in your **Downloads** folder).

### Step 2: Open the app

1. **Double-click `YotoMaker.exe`.**
2. The first time, Windows may show a blue box that says *“Windows protected your
   PC.”* This is normal for new apps. Click the small **“More info”** link, then
   click the **“Run anyway”** button.

   > This happens because the app is brand new and not from a big company. It is
   > safe — it was made just for you.
3. A little music-note icon 🎵 will appear near your clock (bottom-right of the
   screen), and **your web browser will open by itself** showing Yoto Maker.

That's it — it's installed! 🎉

> **Want it to open by itself every time you turn on the computer?**
> Right-click the little 🎵 icon near the clock and click
> **“Start with Windows.”** (Optional — you can always just double-click the app
> when you want it.)

---

## Part 2 — Make your first card

You'll see **four numbered steps** on the screen. Just go top to bottom.

### 1️⃣ Add your audio

- **From YouTube:** copy the link to a video (from your web browser's address
  bar), paste it into the box, and click **Add**. Wait a few seconds while it
  gets the audio.
- **From a file on your computer:** click **Choose an audio file** and pick a
  song or story.

You can add **as many as you like** — each one becomes a track on the card. You
can rename any track by clicking its name, or remove it with the 🗑️ button.

> ✂️ **The “Skip sponsor / ad segments” checkbox** (under the YouTube box) is on
> by default. It automatically cuts out paid promos and “like & subscribe”
> reminders from YouTube audio. Leave it on unless you have a reason not to.

### 2️⃣ Name it & pick a picture

- Type a **name** for the card (for example, *“Bedtime Stories”*).
- Pick a **picture**:
  - **From the audio** — uses the YouTube picture automatically (easiest).
  - **Upload** — choose a photo from your computer.
  - **Icons** — pick a fun little picture.

  The picture shows on your **printed label** and on the **Yoto screen**.

### 3️⃣ Send it to your Yoto

- The first time, click **Connect my Yoto account** and sign in with your normal
  Yoto email and password (in the window that opens). You only do this once.
- Then click the big **Send to Yoto** button and watch the progress bar.
- When it's done, **open the Yoto app on your phone, tap a blank “Make Your Own”
  card** to link it, and press play. 🎶

> ℹ️ If step 3 says it needs to be “set up,” the person who made this for you
> needs to do a quick one-time setup — show them
> **[SETUP-YOTO-CONNECTION.md](SETUP-YOTO-CONNECTION.md)**. Everything else
> (adding audio, pictures, and printing labels) works without it.

### 4️⃣ Print your label

- Click **Make my label**, then **Open / print label**.
- A nice printable card opens in a new tab — press **Print** (Ctrl+P).
- Cut it out and keep it with your Yoto card so you know what's on it!

---

## Doing it again later

- Open Yoto Maker (double-click the app, or click the 🎵 icon near the clock and
  choose **Open Yoto Maker**).
- Click **“Start a new card”** at the bottom if you want to begin fresh.

---

## Questions

**Do I need to keep the app open?**
Only while you're making a card. When you're done you can close the browser tab.
To fully quit, right-click the 🎵 icon near the clock and choose **Quit**.

**Is my Yoto password saved anywhere I should worry about?**
You sign in on Yoto's own website. The app just remembers you're signed in, on
this computer only.

**A YouTube video wouldn't download — why?**
Some videos are blocked or private. Try a different one. Check your internet too.

**Can I use this on a Mac?**
Not yet — this version is for Windows computers.
